Top Visiting Places in Hyderabad

The Charminar is situated on the east bank of Musi River To the west lies the Laad Bazaar, and to the southwest lies the richly ornamented granite Makkah Masjid It is listed as an archaeological and architectural treasure on the official list of monuments prepared by Archaeological Survey India.

Lumbini Park, officially T. Anjaiah Lumbini Park,[1] is a small public, urban park of 3 hectares (7.5 acres) adjacent to Hussain Sagar in Hyderabad, India. Since it is located in the centre of the city and is in close proximity to other tourist attractions, such as Birla Mandir and Necklace Road.

Wonderla Hyderabad offers 28 land based rides and 18 water based attractions on 50 acres of land.[16] Wonderla Amusement Park is located 28 km away from Hyderabad City. Its most notable ride is Recoil, a reverse looping roller coaster

The Statue of Equality, also referred to as the Ramanuja statue, is a statue of the 11th-century vaishnavaite Ramanuja, located on the premises of the Chinna Jeeyar Trust at Muchintal, Ranga Reddy district in the outskirts of Hyderabad.[2] It is the second tallest sitting statue in the world.

Ramoji Film City is an integrated film studio complex located in Hyderabad It is about 30 km away from the city of Hyderabad. it is the largest integrated film city in the world and as such has been certified by Guinness World Records as the largest studio complex

Birla Mandir is a Hindu temple, built on a 280 feet (85 m) high hillock called Naubath Pahad on a 13 acres (53,000 m2) plot in Hyderabad, Telangana, India. The construction took 10 years and was opened in 1976 by Swami Ranganathananda of Ramakrishna Mission.

The architecture of the mini Tirumala temple is also very similar to that of the main Tirumala temple. The idol of Lord Venkateswara is absolutely stunning. As you walk around the main temple,you will notice that even the outside is very similar to the main Tirumala temple

Sanghi Temple, located at Sanghi Nagar in Telangana in India, is about 35 km from Hyderabad city.[1]The sacred Raja Gopuram, which is very tall, can be seen from several kilometers away.The temple is located on the top of Paramanand Giri hill, which attracts a number of devotees.
